Verification is Mirage's trust signal for donors. Verified organizations display a **verified badge** on their public donor reports — independent confirmation that the organization behind the impact story is real and in good standing.

## What you'll need

Documents that establish your organization's legal identity and nonprofit status. Common examples:

- Articles of incorporation
- IRS 501(c)(3) determination letter (or your country's equivalent)
- A recent annual filing such as IRS Form 990

## Submit for verification

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Settings">
    Go to your organization's **Settings** page and find the verification section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload documents">
    Add your documents and, optionally, a note explaining what you've provided (helpful if your documentation differs from the US-centric examples above).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Your status changes to **Pending review** and the Mirage team is notified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get the result">
    Your stewards are notified when review completes. Verified organizations immediately show the badge on donor reports.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Verification statuses

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Not started** | You haven't submitted documents yet. |
| **Pending review** | Documents submitted; the Mirage team is reviewing. |
| **Verified** | You're verified — the badge appears on your donor-facing pages. |
| **Needs attention** | The review couldn't be completed. The notification includes reviewer notes; address them and resubmit. |

<Note>
  Verification is optional — you can run projects, deploy reporters, and share donor reports without it. But the badge meaningfully strengthens the trust story your donor reports tell, so we recommend submitting early.
</Note>
