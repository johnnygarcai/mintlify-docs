## Create your account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign up">
    Go to [app.mirage.partners](https://app.mirage.partners) and sign up with your email address or Google account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Land on the welcome screen">
    New accounts without an organization are guided to create one. If you were invited to an existing organization by email, follow the link in your invitation instead — you'll join that organization directly. See [Team & field reporters](/organization/team-and-field-reporters).

    If your email domain matches an organization already on Mirage, the welcome screen offers to **request an invite** from them instead — a quick way to avoid setting up a duplicate organization for the same nonprofit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create the organization

From the welcome screen (or **New organization** in the dashboard), fill in your organization profile:

- **Name** — how your organization appears everywhere in Mirage, including donor-facing pages.
- **Description** — a short summary of your mission.
- **Website, location, and contact details** — email and phone for your organization.
- **Logo and cover image** — optional, but they make your dashboard and donor-facing pages feel like yours.

The person who creates the organization automatically becomes its first **steward**. You can update any of these details later from your organization's **Settings** page.

## Finding your way around

Once your organization exists, its dashboard is home base:

<Frame caption="The organization overview — getting-started checklist, headline stats, and your projects.">
  <img src="/images/steward-org-overview.png" alt="Steward dashboard organization overview" />
</Frame>

| Area | What you'll find |
| --- | --- |
| **Dashboard** | Project cards, headline metrics, and verification status at a glance. |
| **Projects** | Create and manage impact initiatives. See [Creating projects](/projects/creating-projects). |
| **People** | Team members, email invitations, and reporter invite codes. See [Team & field reporters](/organization/team-and-field-reporters). |
| **Approvals** | The review queue for incoming field reports. See [Reviewing reports](/projects/reviewing-reports). |
| **Donations** | A running ledger of money received from donors, grants, and matches. |

Organization-level **Settings** (profile and [verification](/organization/verification)) and **Plans & billing** live behind a **Settings** button in the Dashboard header rather than the main sidebar.

Each sidebar item shows a small badge when something needs your attention — a dot for items you must act on, a number for unread updates. On the page itself, updates appear as dismissible cards near the top: a new team member joining, a verification result, or a report ready for review.

## Multiple organizations

One account can belong to several organizations (useful for consultants, network organizations, or fiscal sponsors). Use the organization switcher in the sidebar to move between them; each organization's projects, people, and billing are fully separate.

<Note>
  Getting-started checklist for a brand-new organization: create a project, invite reporters, and submit your verification documents. The [Quickstart](/quickstart) covers the first two; [Verification](/organization/verification) covers the third.
</Note>
