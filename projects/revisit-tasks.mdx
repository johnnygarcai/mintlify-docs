Anyone can photograph a well on opening day. **Revisits** prove it's still flowing six months later — scheduled follow-up verifications that turn a one-time claim into evidence of sustained impact.

## Schedule a revisit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create it">
    From the project page, schedule a revisit with a **label** ("6-month check — well functionality") and a **date**. Optionally link it to the **original report** it should verify, so the follow-up evidence can be compared side by side.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Mirage dispatches it automatically">
    On the scheduled date, Mirage creates a reporting task for the revisit. To your reporters it looks like any other task on their dashboard — instructions, questions, evidence requirements.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Completion links back">
    When a reporter submits and the report is finalized, the revisit is marked **completed** and linked to the fulfilling report. The project page shows the full revisit timeline: scheduled, dispatched, completed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Revisit statuses

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Scheduled** | Waiting for its date. |
| **Dispatched** | The reporting task is live in the field. |
| **Completed** | A report came back and closed the loop. |
| **Cancelled** | Withdrawn before completion. |

## Revisits and donor trust

Donor reports incorporate revisit history — completed revisits appear as part of the sustained-impact story. Mirage also computes a project health **verdict** from signals like overdue revisits and metric trends:

| Verdict | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Continue** | Evidence is current and healthy. |
| **Review** | Something needs attention — often overdue revisits. |
| **Pause** | Significant gaps; investigate before making new claims. |

<Tip>
  Schedule revisits at the moment you approve a milestone report — while the context is fresh. A rhythm of 3-, 6-, and 12-month checks on flagship outcomes gives your donor reports a durability story very few organizations can match.
</Tip>
