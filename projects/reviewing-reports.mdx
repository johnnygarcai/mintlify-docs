The **Approvals** page is your organization's review queue. This is where the "steward approved" half of Mirage happens: the AI proposes, you decide.

## What happens when a report comes in

When a reporter submits and finalizes a report, Mirage automatically:

1. **Analyzes the media**: audio is transcribed and language-detected (with an English translation when the original is in another language); photos and video get AI-written summaries.
2. **Extracts metric contributions**: for each of the project's metrics, the AI proposes values it found in the answers and evidence, each with a **confidence score** and a link to the exact question or attachment it came from.
3. **Queues everything for review**: contributions arrive as **pending** and count toward nothing until you act.

## Review a report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the submission">
    From the **Approvals** page, open a finalized report. You'll see the reporter's answers, their attached photos, video, and audio (with transcriptions and AI summaries), and their free-form observation notes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evaluate the contributions">
    Each proposed metric contribution shows the metric, the extracted value, the AI's confidence, and its source. Click through to the source to check the AI's work against the actual evidence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve or reject each one">
    - **Approve**: the value rolls up into the project's aggregated metrics and becomes available to donor reports.
    - **Reject**: the value is excluded. Use this for misreads, duplicates, or evidence that doesn't hold up.
    - Use **Approve all** / **Reject all** to act on every pending contribution for a report at once when they're all clearly good (or clearly not).
  </Step>
</Steps>

Every decision is recorded with who reviewed it and when: your verification audit trail.

## What the reporter sees

Your review is the feedback loop for the field. In the reporter app, each submission shows its status:

| Status | Meaning for the reporter |
| --- | --- |
| **Draft** | Still being written; not yet submitted. |
| **Pending** | Submitted and awaiting steward review. |
| **Accepted** | At least one of its contributions was approved; their work counted. |
| **Declined** | Its contributions were rejected. |

Consistently reviewing promptly keeps reporters engaged: they can see their work landing.

## Consent flags

Photos and video arrive with the consent metadata reporters captured at upload: whether **face permission** was obtained and whether the media is **internal only**. You don't need to police this manually at review time (donor-facing pages exclude non-consented and internal-only media automatically), but the flags are visible during review so you know what will and won't be publicly usable.

Mirage also automatically screens photos for children's faces and blurs them before they can appear on a public page, regardless of the consent flags. A photo can take a few minutes to clear this automatic review; until it does, donor reports and recruitment pages hold it back rather than show it unblurred.

<Tip>
  High-confidence contributions from a reporter with a good track record can usually be approved quickly; spend your review time on low-confidence extractions and unusual values. Confidence scores exist exactly to help you triage.
</Tip>
