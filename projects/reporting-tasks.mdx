A **reporting task** is the unit of work you deploy to the field: a set of instructions, questions, and evidence requirements that reporters respond to. One task can collect many reports, from one reporter over time or a whole cohort at once.

## Create a task

<Frame caption="The new reporting task dialog: title, instructions, capacity, bounty, evidence toggles, and questions.">
  <img src="/images/steward-new-task-form.png" alt="New reporting task dialog with title, instructions, capacity, bounty, evidence, and questions" />
</Frame>

From a project page, create a reporting task with:

- **Title**: what the reporter is being asked to do ("Water quality sample collection").
- **Description**: detailed instructions: what to look for, equipment needed, safety notes. Reporters read this on their phone at the site, so be concrete.
- **Capacity**: the maximum number of reports you want against this task.
- **Bounty**: an optional reward value that signals the task's worth to reporters and is credited toward their earnings when reports are accepted.
- **Evidence requirements**: toggle which media the task calls for: **photo**, **video**, **audio**.
- **Questions**: the structured part of the report.

## Question types

| Type | Reporter sees | Good for |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Short text** | A single-line input | Names, IDs, brief facts |
| **Long text** | A paragraph box | Observations, stories |
| **Number** | A numeric input | Counts and measurements, the raw material for metrics |
| **Yes / No** | Two buttons | Conditions met, boolean-share metrics |
| **Agreement scale** | A 5-point scale (strongly agree → strongly disagree) | Sentiment and satisfaction |
| **Multiple choice** | Radio buttons or checkboxes | Categorical answers with known options |

Mark questions **required** to prevent submission without them; leave nice-to-haves optional so a spotty connection or a rushed visit doesn't block a report.

## What reporters experience

As soon as the task exists in an active project, reporters who belong to your organization see it on their dashboard at [report.mirage.partners](https://report.mirage.partners), with your instructions, the bounty, the evidence checklist, and a map link with directions to the project site. They answer one question at a time with automatic saving, attach media as they go, and submit when done. The full reporter-side flow is documented in [Submitting field reports](/reporters/submitting-field-reports).

## Design tips

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ask for the numbers your metrics need">
    The AI extracts metric values from answers and media. If a metric needs "liters of water distributed", include a number question that asks exactly that.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="One task per activity, not per visit">
    Tasks are reusable: the same task can receive reports week after week. Create a new task when the activity changes, not when the calendar does. For scheduled follow-ups, use [revisit tasks](/projects/revisit-tasks) instead.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Request the evidence you'll want donors to see">
    Photos become the visual heart of donor reports. If you toggle photo evidence on and instruct reporters on framing ("capture the well with the village in the background"), your donor reports will thank you.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep it phone-sized">
    Reporters answer on a phone, possibly offline, possibly in the sun. Ten focused questions beat thirty vague ones.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
