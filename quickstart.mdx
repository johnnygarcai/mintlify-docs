This guide walks the full golden path: create your account and organization, set up a project, deploy a field reporter, review their first report, and share a donor-ready impact page.

## Prerequisites

- A modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge)
- At least one person who can act as a field reporter — a teammate works fine for your first run

## The golden path

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your account">
    Go to [app.mirage.partners](https://app.mirage.partners) and sign up with your email or Google account. First-time users land on a welcome screen that points you to organization setup.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your organization">
    Give your organization a name, description, website, location, and contact details, and upload a logo if you have one handy. You're automatically added as a **steward** — the admin role that manages everything else in this guide.

    See [Create your organization](/organization/create-your-organization) for the full walkthrough.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create your first project">
    From your organization's **Projects** page, create a project: a title, a short summary, a location, and the metrics you want to track (for example "Wells dug" or "% of households with clean water access").

    Metrics are the backbone of everything downstream — [Defining metrics](/projects/defining-metrics) explains the options.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a reporting task">
    Inside the project, create a **reporting task**: the work unit your field reporters respond to. Write clear instructions, add the questions you want answered, and toggle which evidence you need — photos, video, or audio.

    See [Reporting tasks](/projects/reporting-tasks).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Deploy your field reporters">
    From your organization's **People** page, generate a 6-character **invite code** and share it with your reporters — in person, over SMS, or as a printable QR card. Reporters join at [report.mirage.partners](https://report.mirage.partners) by entering the code, and your tasks appear on their dashboard immediately.

    See [Team & field reporters](/organization/team-and-field-reporters).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the first submission">
    When a reporter submits, the report shows up in your organization's **Reporting** queue. Mirage's AI will have already analyzed the evidence and proposed metric values with confidence scores. Review the answers and media, then **approve or reject** each proposed contribution — only approved data counts toward your project metrics.

    See [Reviewing reports](/projects/reviewing-reports).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate and share a donor report">
    From the project page, generate a **donor report**. Mirage assembles your approved metrics, verified photos, and an AI-written narrative into a polished public page. Copy the link and send it to a donor — no login required on their end.

    See [Generating donor reports](/donor-reports/generating-donor-reports).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Check>
  That's the whole loop: evidence in from the field, verified metrics out to donors. Everything else in these docs is depth on the steps above.
</Check>
