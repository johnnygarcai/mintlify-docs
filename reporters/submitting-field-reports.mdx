## Start a report

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a task">
    From your dashboard, tap a task to see its details: the instructions, the reward, the evidence needed (photo, video, or audio), the questions you'll answer, and a **Get directions** button to the site.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Start Reporting">
    A draft report opens. If you've already started one for this task, you'll continue where you left off.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Answer the questions

Questions come one at a time with a progress bar. Your answers **save automatically** as you type — if you're interrupted, close the app, or lose signal, your draft is safe and waiting.

You'll see different answer types: short and long text, numbers, yes/no, a 5-point agreement scale, and multiple choice. Required questions must be answered before you can submit.

## Capture evidence

The **Evidence from the Field** section is where your report earns its credibility:

- **Photos** — take a new one with **Open Camera** or attach from your library. Photos are compressed on your phone before upload, so they work on slow connections.
- **Video** — captured or attached the same way.
- **Audio** — tap **Record Audio** to capture voices, testimony, or ambient context in any language. Mirage transcribes it — and translates it to English — automatically.

### Consent matters

When you add a photo or video of people, the app asks two questions:

- **Face permission obtained?** — did the people pictured agree to appear?
- **Internal use only?** — should this stay out of public donor pages?

Answer honestly. Media without face permission or marked internal-only is still valuable evidence for the organization — it just won't be shown publicly. This protects the people you photograph.

## Add observations and submit

Use the **observation notes** box for anything the questions didn't ask — context, surprises, concerns. Then tap **Complete Report**.

You'll get a confirmation screen with a **confirmation ID** (like `MIR-483-2026`) you can copy or download as a receipt. Your evidence is encrypted and processing begins immediately; extracted metrics typically appear on the organization's side within about 15 minutes.

## After you submit

Track every report on your **Reports** page:

| Status | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Draft** | Not submitted yet — you can keep editing or delete it. |
| **Pending** | Submitted; the organization is reviewing. |
| **Accepted** | Your report was reviewed and your data counted. 🎉 |
| **Declined** | The reviewers couldn't accept it this time. |

## Working with a poor connection

The app is built for the field. Your answers save locally as you go, and uploads retry automatically when connectivity returns. Best practice on unreliable networks: capture everything at the site, and if uploads are struggling, finish submitting when you're back in coverage — your draft holds everything.

<Tip>
  Quality wins. Clear photos that show the work, audio in people's own voices, and specific numbers make your reports more likely to be accepted — and more likely to become the evidence that unlocks funding for the project.
</Tip>
