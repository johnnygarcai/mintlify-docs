Every generated donor report has a **public link**. Anyone with the link can view it: no account, no login, no app install. Copy it from the report and send it however you reach your donors: email, text, a QR code on a printed appeal.

## What donors see

A donor report is a full-page, mobile-friendly impact experience:

- **Hero**: your project's hero image with the AI-written opening narrative.
- **Verified metrics**: each metric with its formatted value and description.
- **Photo gallery**: consented field photos, with AI-written captions from the actual evidence.
- **Community highlights and funnel**: the stories and progression behind the numbers.
- **Call to action**: the next step you want the donor to take.
- **Verified badge**: if your organization has completed [verification](/organization/verification).

The page reads like something a design team spent a week on. Donors don't see the machinery. They see proof.

## Privacy and consent

Donor reports enforce consent automatically:

- Photos and video captured **without face permission** never appear on a public report.
- Media marked **internal only** never appears on a public report.
- Reporters who enabled anonymous mode aren't identified.
- Only steward-approved data feeds the numbers. Pending and rejected contributions stay private.

You never have to hand-filter media before sharing: the flags reporters set at capture time carry through the whole pipeline.

<Note>
  As an additional safeguard, Mirage automatically scans every field photo for children's faces and blurs them before the photo can appear on a public page, regardless of the consent flags a reporter set. A newly uploaded photo is held back from donor reports and recruitment landing pages until this automatic review finishes, which is usually within a few minutes.
</Note>

## Sharing well

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Match the report to the audience">
    Generate a donor-linked report for a major donor (the narrative leads with their priorities) and a general one for newsletters. Reports are cheap to generate, so make more than one.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Choose frozen for the record, live for relationships">
    A frozen snapshot is a stable artifact you can reference later; a live report is a standing window into the project. See [live vs. frozen](/donor-reports/generating-donor-reports#live-vs-frozen).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Anyone with the link can view">
    Treat the URL like a published page, not a private document. Don't include anything in project summaries or metric descriptions you wouldn't want public.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
